# Brand Language Guidelines

## Brand Voice

These guidelines ensure consistent, professional, and customer-friendly language across all content.

## Terms to Avoid

- **Avoid:** "cheap" → Use "affordable"
- **Avoid:** "buy now" → Use "get started"
- **Avoid:** "click here" → Use descriptive link text
- **Avoid:** "guaranteed" → Use "proven"
- **Avoid:** "never" → Use "rarely" or specific timeframes

## Capitalization Rules

- **Capitalize:** Company Names, Product Names, Service Names
- **Capitalize:** Important Brand Terms
- **Capitalize:** Proper Industry Certifications

## SEO-Friendly Alternatives

- **SEO-Friendly:** "professional services" instead of "expert services"
- **SEO-Friendly:** "small business solutions" instead of "SMB solutions"
- **SEO-Friendly:** "customer success stories" instead of "case studies"
- **SEO-Friendly:** "free consultation" instead of "complimentary consultation"

## Preferred Terminology

- **Preferred:** "Our customers" (not "clients" or "users")
- **Preferred:** "Team members" (not "employees" or "staff")
- **Preferred:** "Partnership" (not "vendor relationship")
- **Preferred:** Use active voice over passive voice
- **Preferred:** Use "we help you" instead of "we do for you"

## Tone Guidelines

- Maintain a friendly but professional tone
- Use inclusive language (avoid gendered terms when possible)
- Focus on benefits, not just features
- Use conversational language that sounds natural
- Avoid corporate jargon and buzzwords
